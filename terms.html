1. The app

Rūh is an iOS application for reading, memorizing, and reciting the Qur’an. Features include:

  • Reading the Qur’an offline in a customizable mushaf layout
  • Practicing memorization with ayah-by-ayah and page-by-page views
  • On-device real-time recitation tracking using speech recognition
  • An optional pronunciation review feature that uploads a single verse’s audio to our server for feedback
  • Progress tracking, bookmarks, and session history
  • Optional account creation for syncing data across devices

See our Privacy Policy for how we handle data, including the clear separation between on-device processing and the optional pronunciation review upload.

5. The pronunciation review feature

Because the pronunciation review (“Muaalem”) feature is different from most app features — it uploads audio to a server and returns a subjective feedback output — the following additional terms apply to it specifically.

5.1 It is a practice aid, not a certification

The pronunciation review feature is a practice tool, not a religious certification. It uses a machine-learning model that can be wrong, miss mistakes, or flag correct recitations as mistakes. If the feature and your teacher disagree, trust your teacher. The feature is designed to help you notice things to practice, not to declare anything right or wrong.

5.2 It is not religious scholarship

Nothing produced by the pronunciation review feature should be taken as religious scholarship, a fatwa, or a teaching credential. The app does not issue ijāzahs. The app does not replace a qualified teacher (shaykh).

5.3 You opt in each time, and you can turn it off

The first time you use pronunciation review, we show you a consent screen explaining exactly what is about to happen with your audio. If you proceed, subsequent uses may not show the same consent screen each time — but you can always turn the entire feature off in Settings, and you can always choose not to tap the review button.

5.4 We don’t store the audio

Our server processes the audio in memory and discards it immediately after analysis. We do not keep the raw audio. We do keep the analysis output (which letters the model flagged, for example) so you can review past sessions within your account.

See the Privacy Policy §2.5 for the full technical description.
