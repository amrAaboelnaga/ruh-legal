Privacy Policy

Last updated: 21 April 2026

This policy explains what information the Rūh iOS application (“Rūh”, “the app”) collects, how it is used, and the controls you have. Rūh is operated by Amr Aboelnaga, based in the Commonwealth of Virginia, United States. You can reach us at amr@verific.ai.

1. Who this policy applies to

Rūh is offered to users in the United States and other non-EU, non-UK, non-Swiss jurisdictions. It is not offered to users in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland. If you access Rūh from one of those regions, please do not use the app.

2. Information we collect

2.1 Information stored on your device

Most of what Rūh stores lives only on your iPhone and never leaves it unless you explicitly enable Cloud Sync (§3) or Pronunciation Review (§4). This includes:

  • Your reciter choice, theme, daily goal, and other preferences.
  • Your bookmarks and ayah notes.
  • Your memorisation (hifz) entries and practice history.
  • Your khatam plans and daily reading log.
  • Cached Qur’an audio files and mushaf rendering caches.

This on-device data can be deleted at any time by signing out of your account (if you created one) or by deleting the app.

2.2 Microphone audio (on-device recitation tracking)

When you start recitation tracking, Rūh accesses your microphone so it can follow along with your reading in real time. Audio processed by the on-device recitation tracker is never uploaded. It exists only in memory while you are reciting and is discarded immediately.

2.3 Account information (optional)

If you sign in with Apple, Apple shares with us a unique user identifier and, the first time you sign in, your name and a (real or relay) email address. We store these in our Supabase database to create your profile. No password is stored or handled by us.

2.4 Cloud-synced content (opt-in)

When you are signed in and Cloud Sync is enabled, the following data is uploaded to our Supabase backend so it can travel with you across devices: profile fields (display name, age range, reading goal), bookmarks, hifz entries with practice results, khatam plans, settings, and ayah notes. We do not sync your microphone audio, your reading-tracker history, or any device identifiers under this flow.

2.5 Pronunciation Review audio (opt-in)

If you explicitly opt in to Pronunciation Review and request feedback on a specific ayah, the recorded audio for that ayah is sent to our GPU service (operated on Modal.com) for analysis. After the analysis result is returned, the audio is discarded. It is not stored, aggregated, or used to train any model. If this toggle is off in Settings, no audio is ever transmitted.

2.6 Local notifications

Rūh schedules notification reminders entirely on-device using Apple’s local notifications API. No push tokens are collected and no notifications are sent from a server.

2.7 Information we do not collect

  • We do not track you across other apps or websites.
  • We do not serve advertising and have no ad SDKs embedded.
  • We do not collect location data.
  • We do not access your contacts, photos, or calendar.

3. How information is used

Information collected is used solely to:

  • Provide and improve the core reading and memorisation features.
  • Sync your data across devices when you are signed in.
  • Return pronunciation feedback when you request it.
  • Respond to your support emails.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We do not use your data for advertising.

4. Who processes your data on our behalf

  • Supabase — managed Postgres database and authentication provider, hosted in the United States. Your profile and cloud-synced content live here when Cloud Sync is enabled.
  • Modal Labs — ephemeral GPU compute provider, used only when you submit an ayah for Pronunciation Review. Audio is processed in memory and discarded.
  • Apple — Sign in with Apple, local notifications, iCloud Keychain (for secure session tokens if you use it).
  • Expo Application Services — used for over-the-air updates and crash diagnostics (only when Crash Diagnostics is on).

5. Your controls

  • Opt-in toggles for Pronunciation Review, Cloud Sync, and Crash Diagnostics live in Settings → About and Settings → Pronunciation review.
  • Export: if you are signed in you can request a complete copy of your cloud-synced data from Settings → Account → Export my data. The app produces a JSON file you can save or share anywhere.
  • Delete account: Settings → Account → Delete my account removes your profile and every server-side row tied to your user ID. On-device data is also wiped on the device you triggered the deletion from. This action is permanent.
  • Sign out: clears your local account state and wipes app data on this device. A different user can then sign in to a clean slate.

6. Data retention

Cloud-synced data is retained for as long as you have an account. When you delete your account, your server rows are deleted promptly. An anonymised hash of your deletion event is kept in an audit log so we can honour the deletion if the same Apple ID is used to create a new account in the future.

Pronunciation Review audio is never retained. Log entries for the GPU service retain only the non-content fields needed to diagnose problems (request timestamp, duration, success/failure, anonymised user hash) and are rotated out within 30 days.

7. Children

Rūh is intended for users aged 13 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will remove it.

8. Security

On-device data uses the iOS sandbox. Session tokens are stored in the iOS Keychain. Data in transit to our backends is encrypted with TLS. Supabase row-level security policies ensure a signed-in user can only read and write their own rows. No system is perfectly secure; if you believe your account has been compromised, contact us immediately.

9. California residents (CCPA/CPRA)

Californians may request to know what personal information we hold about them, request deletion, and request correction. You can exercise these rights through Settings → Account → Export my data and Settings → Account → Delete my account, or by emailing amr@verific.ai. We do not sell personal information and we do not “share” it for cross-context behavioural advertising.

10. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes in any material way, the “Last updated” date above will change and the app will surface a short disclosure the next time you open Settings → About. Continued use of Rūh after such a change means you accept the revised policy.

11. Contact

Questions about this policy, or requests to exercise your rights, should go to amr@verific.ai.
