Privacy Policy

Last updated: 21 April 2026 Effective date: [TBD]

This is the privacy policy for Rūh (“the app”, “we”, “us”, “our”), an iOS application for reading, memorizing, and reciting the Qur’an. The app is currently published by Amr Aboelnaga (“the developer”), an individual sole proprietor based in the Commonwealth of Virginia, United States of America. A limited liability company is planned to take over publishing the app; when it is formed, this policy will be updated with the LLC’s legal name and jurisdiction, with advance notice to account users per §10.

We’ve written this in plain language. If anything is unclear, contact us at amr@verific.ai. A formal “legal” version of these terms governs in case of dispute, and is identical in substance.

TL;DR

  • The app works without an account for reading and memorization. Anyone of any age can open the app and start reading.
  • An account is optional, and is required only for: cloud sync across devices, the pronunciation review feature (“Muaalem”), and any future social or community features.
  • Accounts can be created with Sign in with Apple, Sign in with Google, or an email and password. You must be 13 or older to create an account.
  • Your microphone is used to track your recitation on your device only. Nothing is uploaded for that feature.
  • One specific feature, pronunciation review (Muaalem), sends a single verse’s audio to our server for analysis when you ask for it. You opt in the first time, and you can turn it off any time. The recording is processed in memory, analyzed, and discarded within the same request. It is never written to disk, never logged, and never backed up. Only the analysis output (which letters, if any, the model thinks may have been mispronounced) is kept on your account so you can review your past sessions.
  • We do not sell your data. We do not share it with advertisers. We do not use your recitation, your reading habits, or anything else to train any AI model — ours or anyone else’s.
  • v1 availability is deliberately limited. We currently ship the app to the United States and selected non-EU markets. The European Union, European Economic Area (Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway), United Kingdom, and Switzerland are not available in v1 — these markets will be added in a future release once EU/UK data-protection representatives are in place. If you are reading this privacy policy from an excluded market, you are not currently our user; the GDPR / UK GDPR sections below describe how we will handle your data when availability is restored.
  • You have full rights under CCPA (California), BIPA (Illinois), and other US state privacy laws. We honor GDPR-level rights globally as a matter of practice, even where local law doesn’t require it.

1. Who this policy applies to

This policy applies to anyone who uses the app, with two distinct cases:

  • Guest users — anyone who opens the app without creating an account. Guest users can read the Qur’an, use memorization features, and use real-time on-device recitation tracking. Guest data is stored only on the device.
  • Account users — anyone aged 13 or older who creates an account to enable cloud sync, the Muaalem pronunciation review feature, or future account-tied features.

If you are under 13, you may use the app as a guest. You may not create an account. The app does not knowingly collect any personally identifiable information from users under 13. See §8 for details.

2. Information we collect

2.1 Guest-mode information (stored only on your device)

When you use the app without an account, the following data is stored locally on your device using standard iOS storage. We never see it, it never leaves your phone, and uninstalling the app deletes all of it permanently.

  • App settings (theme, font sizes, reciter choice, language preference, accessibility options)
  • Memorization progress (which verses you’ve practiced, when, your weak spots, hifz cycles)
  • Reading progress (last page, bookmarks, reading streak, khatam tracking)
  • Downloaded recitation audio
  • Tajweed sensitivity preference

If you later decide to create an account, you can choose to upload this local data to your new account so it syncs to your other devices. That migration is one-time and explicit — we don’t reach into your phone.

2.2 Account information (only when you create an account)

When you create an account, we collect the following from your chosen sign-in method:

Sign-in method What we receive
Sign in with Apple A unique Apple-issued user identifier; your name (only if you choose to share it on the Apple consent screen); a relay email (*@privaterelay.appleid.com) which forwards to your real address but doesn’t reveal it to us.
Sign in with Google A unique Google-issued user identifier; your real email address; your name; your Google profile photo (if you have one).
Email and password Your email address, your chosen display name, and a password (which we never store in plain text — it’s hashed on our authentication provider).

We may also generate and store on our servers:

  • A unique user ID for your account in our system
  • The date your account was created
  • The date you last signed in
  • Your account preferences (theme, daily goal, etc. — synced from device settings)

We do not collect:

  • Your phone number
  • Your physical location (we use your timezone for prayer-time–style features only, and that’s derived from your device, not GPS)
  • Your contacts
  • Your photos
  • Advertising identifiers
  • Cross-app tracking data (IDFA)
  • Any biometric identifier

2.3 Sync data (only when you have an account)

When you are signed in, the following data is synchronized between your devices through our cloud storage:

  • Memorization progress (hifz state, weak ayahs, mutashabihat practice)
  • Mushaf bookmarks and last-read position
  • Reading streak and khatam (completion cycle) tracking
  • App settings (theme, font sizes, reciter choice, accessibility preferences)
  • Daily-goal progress

Sync is on by default for account users. You can pause sync from Settings.

2.4 Audio processed on your device only — never collected

The app uses your microphone in two distinct ways. The first is fully on-device:

  • Real-time recitation tracking. When you start a recitation session in the practice arena or mushaf view, the app uses your microphone to recognize Arabic words as you recite them, so that we can highlight words and follow your position in the verse. This processing happens entirely on your iPhone using a machine-learning model bundled inside the app. The audio stream is held only in memory for the duration of the recitation, then discarded the moment you stop. It is never recorded to disk, never uploaded, and never seen by us. This applies whether or not you have an account.

2.5 Audio sent to our server — only when you ask, only one verse at a time

The second microphone use is the pronunciation review feature (also called “Muaalem” in the interface). This feature is optional, account-required, and explicitly opt-in.

  • The first time you tap the pronunciation review button, the app shows you a consent screen explaining exactly what’s about to happen, asking for your confirmation. The two buttons are equal weight. There is no dark pattern.
  • If you confirm, the recording for that single verse is sent over an encrypted connection (HTTPS, TLS 1.2 or higher) to a server operated by the developer for pronunciation analysis.
  • The server returns a per-letter analysis of your pronunciation. The analysis output (which letters the model flagged, if any) is saved to your account so you can review your past sessions. The raw audio is not saved — only the analysis.
  • Because the feature requires an account, the recording is associated with your user ID for the duration of the analysis and any subsequent retention period.
  • The recording itself is ephemeral. It is held in memory on our server for the duration of the analysis request, processed, and released from memory as soon as the response is returned. We do not intentionally write the recording to durable storage (disk, database, object storage, or backups), and our server’s logging is configured to capture only the surah and ayah numbers and the analysis outcome — never the audio bytes, audio metadata, or file names. See §5 for the retention table.
  • The recording is never used to train any AI model, ours or anyone else’s, unless you separately and explicitly opt in to such use. There is no such opt-in offered today.
  • The recording is never shared with any third party other than the cloud infrastructure providers required to operate the feature (see §4).

You can use the entire app — including memorization practice and on-device recitation tracking — without ever using pronunciation review.

You can turn off pronunciation review entirely from Settings, in which case no audio will ever leave your device under any circumstances.

2.6 Diagnostic and crash data

The app may collect anonymized crash reports and basic operational diagnostics (e.g., “the app crashed on screen X at time Y”) to help us fix bugs. This data is not linked to your account or device identity. You can opt out of diagnostic collection from Settings.

2.7 What we don’t collect, ever

We do not collect:

  • Your physical location coordinates
  • Your contacts
  • Your photos or camera roll
  • Advertising identifiers
  • Cross-app tracking data
  • Biometric data (other than the strictly-on-device, never-stored audio in §2.4)
  • Any data from third-party advertising networks
  • Anything used to build a behavioral profile of you

3. How we use the information

The on-device data described in §2.1 is used solely to make the app work for you on your device.

The account data in §2.2 is used solely to give you a working account, sync your data across your devices, and let you sign back in.

The sync data in §2.3 is used solely to keep your devices consistent. We do not analyze it for any purpose other than syncing.

The audio in §2.5 is used solely to compute the pronunciation analysis you requested for the verse you submitted, and to return that analysis to your phone.

We do not use any data for advertising, behavioral profiling, ad targeting, or training machine learning models. We do not run any analytics suite that builds a profile of you.

6. Your rights

Regardless of where you live, we honor the following rights for everyone:

  • Right to access — You can request a copy of all data we hold about you.
  • Right to correct — You can correct anything inaccurate.
  • Right to delete — Once account creation is available in the app, you will be able to delete your account directly from Settings → Account → Delete account. Server-side data is then deleted per §5. In the meantime (before accounts ship), there is no account to delete, and all data lives only on your device.
  • Right to export — Once account creation is available, you will be able to request a portable copy of your data in a common machine-readable format from Settings → Account → Export my data.
  • Right to object and restrict — You can opt out of optional data flows (Muaalem, sync, crash diagnostics) without losing the ability to use the rest of the app.
  • Right to withdraw consent — Where we rely on your consent, you can withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing before the withdrawal.
  • Right to complain — If you live in the EU/EEA or UK, you can complain to your local Data Protection Authority. We’d prefer you contact us first so we can fix the problem.

To exercise any of these rights, write to amr@verific.ai. We will respond within 30 days (or the time required by your local law, whichever is shorter).

6.3 Specific notes for Illinois users (BIPA)

Illinois’s Biometric Information Privacy Act (740 ILCS 14/) treats voice recordings used to identify an individual as “biometric identifiers” and requires (a) written informed consent before collection, (b) a published retention schedule and destruction timeline, and (c) a written policy governing the data.

  • Our use of voice recordings is narrow and purpose-limited: the pronunciation review feature processes your audio in real time to produce a pronunciation analysis. We do not use voice data to identify you, to build a voiceprint, or to verify your identity. Your identity is established through your account, not your voice.
  • Consent: before your first use of the pronunciation review feature, the app shows you a consent screen explaining that the audio will be sent to our server, analyzed, and discarded. You must tap “Send this verse” to proceed. This is the “written release” required by BIPA §15(b)(3) — under Illinois case law (see Rosenbach v. Six Flags), an in-app click-through constitutes a signed release for this purpose.
  • Retention schedule: voice recordings are retained for the duration of the analysis request only (typically a few seconds) and are then destroyed by being released from memory. No voice recording is retained longer than the single HTTP request that carried it. This is the published destruction schedule required by BIPA §15(a).
  • Written policy: this section of the Privacy Policy is that written policy.
  • Additional restrictions under BIPA §15(c) and §15(d): we do not profit from, sell, lease, or otherwise profit from voice data, and we do not disclose it except to the developer-operated processor that runs the analysis model.

If you are an Illinois resident and have questions about how BIPA applies to your use of this app, please contact us at amr@verific.ai.

7. Security

We take security seriously, but no system is unbreakable. Here’s what we do and what you should know:

  • All network traffic between the app and our servers uses HTTPS with TLS 1.2 or higher.
  • Passwords (when using the email/password sign-in option) are hashed by Supabase Auth using bcrypt. We never see your plain-text password, not even at the moment of sign-up.
  • Audio uploaded for pronunciation review is held in memory only for the duration of the analysis request and is never written to durable storage, so there is no at-rest encryption to describe — the data does not come to rest.
  • Account access is protected by your chosen sign-in method’s protections (Apple ID, Google account, or your password).
  • We do not contain any cryptography subject to U.S. export restriction; the app uses only standard iOS HTTPS.

If we ever experience a security incident affecting your data, we will notify the relevant supervisory authorities and affected users within the timeframe required by applicable law. Specifically: under GDPR Article 33, we will notify the competent supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of a personal-data breach and, under Article 34, notify affected individuals without undue delay where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights. In other jurisdictions — including California, New York, Illinois, and the various U.S. state data-breach notification laws — we will comply with each jurisdiction’s specific notification window and content requirements (which vary from “without undue delay” to 30–60 days).

8. Children’s privacy

The app is rated 4+ in the App Store because the content (the Qur’an itself) is appropriate for all ages. However, account creation is restricted to users aged 13 or older.

  • Under 13s can use the app as guests. They can read, navigate the mushaf, practice memorization, and use the on-device recitation tracking. Nothing they do is collected or transmitted off the device.
  • Under 13s cannot create an account, cannot use Muaalem (pronunciation review), and cannot use cloud sync. These features all require an account, and account creation requires self-attestation that you are 13 or older.
  • We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children under 13. If we discover that we have inadvertently received such information (for example, because a child lied about their age during signup), we will delete it from our systems as soon as we become aware.
  • If you are a parent or guardian and you believe your child under 13 has created an account in violation of this policy, please contact us at amr@verific.ai and we will delete the account and all associated data immediately.
  • We do not direct the app at children. Children are welcome to use the guest experience, but the app is not designed primarily for children, and we do not market it as a kids’ product.
  • COPPA compliance: Because we do not knowingly collect data from children under 13, COPPA’s parental-consent requirements do not apply. The age gate is the safeguard.
